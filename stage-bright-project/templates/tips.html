General Tips

Know Your Audience: Cater your speech directly to your audience. Think about what previous knowledge or perceptions your audience already has about your topic.


Be Wary with Data: Assume your audience has a 5-second attention span for understanding data. If your data slide takes longer to understand, consider a bullet-point summary instead.


Tell a Story: Wherever you can, implement a narrative. Instead of listing the results of your experiment, describe the process you took to get those results.


Summarize Key Points: Dedicate a slide to summarizing the main points of your speech. Go over the key points you want your audience to remember.


Speech Checklist: the next time you find yourself giving a speech, ask yourself if you are doing all of the following things:

  • Are you making good eye contact with everyone in the room? hint: try looking at peoples' foreheads instead of directly into their eyes!
  • How well are you projecting your voice? Are you too quiet? Too loud?
  • What is your body language like? Is it open? Closed? Don't forget to stand up straight!
  • How fast are you speaking? Are you rushing through your speech? Are you taking enough pauses throughout your speech?
  • What kind of attitude are you displaying? Are you excited and passionate about what you are speaking about?

If you follow all of these tips, you will notice immediate improvement in your public speaking!
